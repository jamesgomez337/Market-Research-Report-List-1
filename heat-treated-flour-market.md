<p><p>The heat-treated flour market consists of two primary segments: dry and wet. Dry heat-treated flour is processed using high temperatures in an oven or dryer, enhancing its microbial safety and baking properties while retaining its dry state. In contrast, wet heat-treated flour involves steaming or boiling, resulting in a moist product that may alter texture and functionality. Both types cater to various culinary applications, including baking, pasta production, and food manufacturing, providing distinct advantages based on desired outcomes.</p></p>
